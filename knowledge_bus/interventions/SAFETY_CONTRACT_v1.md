# Safety Contract v1

Version: **1.0.1**

This contract governs intervention outputs generated by the deterministic intervention evidence layer.

## Scope

Allowed safety classes:

- `lifestyle`
- `monitoring`
- `clinician_referral`

Out of scope:

- prescribing or dosage advice
- diagnosis confirmation
- physical-exam-dependent instructions
- supplement recommendations
- product, brand, or programme promotion

## Denylist Controls

All intervention text fields (`title`, `body`, `why_this_matters`, `evidence_summary`) and clinician templates must be screened against `safety_rules_v1.yaml`.

Critical denylist constraints in v1.0.1:

- `take` alone is **not** a deny phrase
- `treat` alone is **not** a deny phrase
- denylist explicitly includes:
  - `take medication`
  - `take this drug`
  - `take the pill`
  - `take a supplement`
  - `this will treat`
  - `we can treat`
  - `reverse`
  - and other prohibited phrases encoded in YAML

## Confidence and Escalation Behaviour

- Lifestyle and monitoring actions are suppressed when `signal_confidence < 0.40`.
- Clinician referral is never confidence-gated when escalation is required.
- Low-confidence fallback monitoring action must be exactly:
  - `Insufficient data to generate reliable recommendations. Retest recommended in 8–12 weeks with a complete panel.`
- Low-confidence escalation wording must include:
  - `Some markers needed to fully assess this signal are missing from your panel.`

## Ordering and Caps

Ordering is deterministic and defined in `safety_rules_v1.yaml` (`selection_ordering` block).

Caps:

- max 5 interventions per report
- max 2 interventions per system
- clinician referral survives caps (drop non-referral items first)

## Text Limits

Text limits are controlled by YAML rules. v1.0.1 includes:

- `why_this_matters` max length = 200

## Contract modification governance

Any modification to this contract requires:

1) HIGH-risk SOP work package classification
2) dual review (execution + audit) with explicit architecture signoff
3) version bump in both Markdown contract and YAML safety rules
