## Clinical rationale

**3. Clinical rationale**

**What disease/dysfunction does it predict?**  
This bundle is designed to detect *early metabolic dysfunction consistent with insulin resistance* and to stratify risk of progression to **type 2 diabetes** and related cardiometabolic outcomes (metabolic syndrome, atherosclerotic cardiovascular risk). ţłÇciteţłéturn33view0ţłéturn12view0ţłéturn15view0ţłéturn8view0ţłü

**Pathophysiological mechanism (why these signals move early)**  
Insulin resistance is characterised by reduced responsiveness of liver, skeletal muscle, and adipose tissue to insulin. Clinically, this often shows up first as a need for higher insulin secretion to maintain ÔÇťnormalÔÇŁ glucose, and as atherogenic dyslipidaemia (notably higher triglycerides) that reflects impaired lipid handling and hepatic insulin resistance. ţłÇciteţłéturn30search1ţłéturn33view0ţłéturn12view0ţłü

**At what stage does this provide early warning?**  
The intent is to flag risk *before overt dysglycaemia* (i.e., while fasting glucose and/or HbA1c can still be ÔÇťnormalÔÇŁ), by using the **triglycerideÔÇôglucose index (TyG)**ÔÇöa published, reproducible composite signal that predicts incident diabetes in normoglycaemic cohorts and adds information beyond fasting glucose alone in at least one long-term European cohort. ţłÇciteţłéturn12view0ţłéturn33view0ţłéturn3view0ţłü

**Why this matters for health outcomes**  
Even intermediate dysglycaemia (ÔÇťprediabetesÔÇŁ) is associated with higher risks of all-cause mortality and incident cardiovascular disease in large-scale meta-analytic evidence, so earlier detection and risk reduction is clinically meaningful. ţłÇciteţłéturn8view0ţłéturn31search0ţłü

**6. Calculation method**

**Published algorithm (core): TriglycerideÔÇôGlucose Index (TyG)**

**Unit handling (en-GB friendly):**

- glucose mg/dL = glucose (mmol/L) ├Ś 18
