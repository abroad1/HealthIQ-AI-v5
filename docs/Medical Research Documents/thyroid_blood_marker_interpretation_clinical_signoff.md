# Thyroid Blood Marker Interpretation Patterns — Clinical Sign-Off Report

Below is the clinical sign-off on the four proposed thyroid patterns in the attached thyroid batch. The draft entries are directionally sensible, but they need stricter gating than the current wording implies. In routine thyroid testing, TSH is the key first-line discriminator, with FT4 and sometimes FT3 used to classify the pattern; low-T3 patterns are common in non-thyroidal illness; and assay/medication confounders such as biotin, amiodarone, glucocorticoids, and thyroid-hormone therapy can materially distort interpretation.

| Pattern | Medically coherent? | Safe to use cautiously? | Required supporting markers | Required caveats | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free T3 high → T3-predominant thyrotoxicosis context | Yes | Yes, but only if **TSH is suppressed** and FT4 is reviewed | **TSH mandatory**; FT4 strongly recommended; repeat test if discordant | Do not activate from isolated FT3 high. Non-suppressed TSH should trigger discordant-pattern handling, not a thyrotoxic interpretation. Check biotin, assay interference, T3/T4 therapy timing, and amiodarone context. | **Approved with caveats; block unless TSH is available** |
| Free T3 low → low T3 syndrome / non-thyroidal illness context | Yes | Only conditionally | **TSH mandatory**; **FT4 mandatory**; illness / catabolic / medication context required; repeat after recovery preferred | Isolated low FT3 is too non-specific for active thyroid reasoning. Low-T3 patterns are common in systemic illness, calorie deprivation, major surgery, renal failure, and with glucocorticoids/amiodarone. Do not use it to imply hypothyroidism by itself. | **Blocked unless TSH + FT4 + illness/context are available** |

Check biotin/interference, levothyroxine timing or exogenous T4, and amiodarone. | **Approved with caveats; block unless TSH is available** |

## Overall verdict

**APPROVE_SUBSET**

Approve:
- **FT3 high** with mandatory TSH gating
- **FT4 high** with mandatory TSH gating
- **FT4 low** with mandatory TSH gating

Hold back:
- **FT3 low** unless the system can require **TSH + FT4 + illness/medication context** or equivalent recovery/repeat context.

## TSH requirement

**TSH should be mandatory before all four thyroid patterns are activated.**

For **FT3 low**, TSH alone is still not enough; FT4 plus illness/medication context should also be required.

Routine thyroid-testing guidance uses TSH as the key first-line discriminator, with FT4 and FT3 used to classify the abnormal pattern, and primary hypothyroid patterning specifically requires both TSH and FT4.
